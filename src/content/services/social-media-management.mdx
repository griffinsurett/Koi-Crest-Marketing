## The Account That Went Silent

Another week passed. The last post was three weeks ago. Notifications pile up unanswered. The content calendar exists somewhere, unused. Social media has become another item on the endless to-do list that never gets done.

You know consistency matters. You know engagement requires presence. You know social media works for other businesses. But between serving customers, managing operations, and everything else demanding attention—social media keeps falling through the cracks.

The result: an audience that forgets you exist. An algorithm that buries your content. A competitor who shows up every day, slowly capturing the attention you're abandoning.

## Why Social Media Management Fails In-House

Social media looks easy until you try to do it well. The reality of consistent, strategic social presence is more demanding than most businesses anticipate.

**Consistency requires commitment.** Algorithms reward regular posting. Sporadic presence destroys reach. Maintaining consistency while running a business is genuinely difficult.

**Each platform is a full-time job.** Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, Facebook—each has different best practices, formats, and audiences. Doing all of them well requires expertise most businesses don't have.

**Community management takes time.** Social media is social. Comments need responses. Messages need replies. Engagement requires engagement. It's an ongoing conversation, not a broadcast.

**Strategy separates success from noise.** Posting without purpose generates activity, not results. Strategic social media requires planning, analysis, and continuous optimization.
