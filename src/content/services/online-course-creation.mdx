## Knowledge That Doesn't Scale

You know things that others need to learn. Expertise developed over years. Processes refined through experience. Insights that would save others the mistakes you made.

You share it one-on-one. Client sessions. Employee training. Consulting calls. Each time, you explain the same concepts, answer the same questions, walk through the same processes.

Your expertise is valuable. But your time is limited. The gap between what you know and how many people can access it—that's the opportunity.

Online courses transform knowledge into scalable assets. Teach once, reach thousands. Your expertise working while you sleep.

## Why Most Courses Disappoint

Online courses are everywhere—which means bad online courses are everywhere. Understanding what separates effective courses from abandoned ones helps avoid the common failures.

**Information dumps.** Lectures recorded without instructional design. Content that's technically accurate but pedagogically ineffective. Students who watch but don't learn.

**No engagement.** Passive video consumption without interaction. No exercises, no feedback, no application. Courses that people buy but don't complete.

**Poor production.** Bad audio, dim lighting, chaotic slides. Technical quality that undermines expertise perception.

**Missing the business model.** Great content without clear positioning, pricing, or marketing. Courses that exist but don't sell.
