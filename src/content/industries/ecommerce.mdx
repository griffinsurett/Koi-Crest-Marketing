## They Bought From Someone Else

Yesterday, someone searched for exactly what you sell. They compared options, read reviews, weighed alternatives. They were ready to buy.

They didn't buy from you.

Maybe they never found you. Maybe they found you but something felt off—the site, the photos, the messaging. Maybe a competitor simply showed up first, looked more legitimate, and captured the sale before you entered consideration.

This happens hundreds of times daily. Your product might be superior. That advantage means nothing if they buy before they discover you.

## The Brutal Arithmetic of Customer Acquisition

Ecommerce profitability hinges on a single relationship: what you spend to acquire a customer versus what that customer spends with you. Everything else is details.

**Acquisition costs only rise.** Facebook, Instagram, Google—every platform trends more expensive over time. The brands that survive have figured out how to acquire customers profitably even as costs increase. This requires both efficiency and lifetime value optimization.

**First-time buyers are the most expensive.** Converting a stranger costs five to ten times more than selling again to someone who's already purchased. Yet most brands pour resources into acquisition while neglecting the customers already in their database.

**Discovery is fragmented.** Your potential customers encounter brands across dozens of touchpoints—social feeds, search results, email, influencers, word-of-mouth. Showing up once isn't enough. Presence must be consistent across the channels where your customers actually spend attention.

**Brand is your moat.** Anyone can sell a similar product. What they can't replicate is the relationship you build with customers—the trust, the story, the identity that makes people choose you even when alternatives exist.
